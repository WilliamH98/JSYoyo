1. Bring back your throw hand, holding the yo-yo facing backwards.

2. Swing your throw arm forward while releasing the yo-yo.

3. Let it swing...

4. ...forward...

5. ...upwards...

6. ...until it is level with your hand.

7. Now give it a tug and let it come back...

8. ...while turning your palm upwards.

9. Catch it...

10. ...and you have the yo-yo in your hand ready for the next throw. The palm has to be facing upwards, not only to make the catch easier, but also to have the yo-yo oriented correctly. If you catch it the other way round, you would have to turn it before throwing again.
