1. Start as with a Forward Pass, by throwing the yo-yo...

2. ...forward. In this picture the yo-yo rotates clockwise!

3. It will usually sleep for a fraction of a second..

4. before being tugged back towards the player.

5. When it comes back, ...

6. ...you do not catch it, but you bring your hand down, letting the yo-yo swing over...

7. ...and around it.

8. While the yo-yo rounds your throw hand, flick the hand forward, slinging the yo-yo away from you again. The yo-yo will now start to unwind again, changing direction of spin.

9. This is the moment, where the high speed camera comes in handy (steps 8, 9, and 10 are only a 60th of a second apart), as it captures the moment, where the yo-yo flips around. You read correctly, the yo-yo flips around. Until now, the white side of the yo-yo was facing us, now the dark side is beginning to show (pun intended.)

10. The yo-yo has completed its flip, and is now rotating clockwise once again.

11. Once more sling the yo-yo forward...

12. ...up...

13. ...up...

14. ...and back towards you. Note, that the yo-yo should be slanted slightly sideways. Do not panic, it is supposed to do this, it actually helps with the flipping around on the next loops.

15. Once more, sling the yo-yo...

16. ...around your throw hand.

17. Here's the flip again. The dark side makes way for the white side. :)

18. Down...

19. ...forward...

20. ...and, once you have done enough loops, you can catch the yo-yo from below.



Variations

--> Three Leaf Clover - Throw the first loop 45 degrees upwards, the second one level, and the third one 45 degrees downwards. Catch.
--> Outside Loops - Basically the same as Loop the Loop/Inside Loops, only the yo-yo is slung around the outside of your throw arm. This can be alternated with inside loops.
--> Hop the Fence Loops - Looping downwards, a bit like Hop the Fence.
